- fulsome
  - 发音
    - 英 /'fulsəm/
    - 美 /'fʊlsəm/
  - 词义
    - adj. 因过分而显得可厌的
      - If you describe expressions of praise, apology, or gratitude as fulsome, you disapprove of them because they are exaggerated and elaborate, so that they sound insincere
  - 记忆
    - ful (看作full， 满的) + some (…的) → 太满的 → 因过分而显得可厌的
  - 同根词
    - adv. fulsomely 过分地；令人作呕地
    - n. fulsomeness 虚情；谄媚
  - 近义词
    - adj. 令人生厌的；过度的
      - unreasonable
      - hohum
  - 例句
    - Newspapers have been fulsome in their praise of the former president. 报界对前任总统的赞扬过于恭维了。
  - 补充
